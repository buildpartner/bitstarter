FAQ

What does it do?
Enables clear control of multiple cloud environments, with individual saved views. Maintains simplicity through leveraging existing systems and a modular plugin approach.
What do I need it for?
Minimise multiple complicated interfaces, centralise monitoring for helpdesk (flightdeck), enable users for self-orchestration...
Who are we?
Dashscape is an Open Source project initiated by Marten Hauville and buildpartner. Experience of more than 20 years in IT industry, over 8 years building and delivering virtual environments, applications and managed services has identified the need to make hybrid and private cloud management easier.
What is your experience?
Marten Hauville successfully delivered business technology solutions across multiple business areas, working with product development, infrastructure engineers, developers and C level decision makers. Designing, building and delivering infrastructure, systems and applications for the enterprise market, for customers such as Cisco, HSBC, ING, Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA), Australian Securities Exchange (ASX), Telstra and SingTel Optus with experience as an Engineer, Architect, Project Manager and Technical Business Manager. See more at Marten Hauville on LinkedIn.
Why are you doing this?
For the past year Marten has been building OpenStack private clouds in Europe and Australia, as well as working with Rackspace and AWS public cloud environments. One thing is clear, the interfaces and management tools for these environments are unbearably complicated and disparate, despite the many commercial platforms out there. It doesn't need to be this way. The resounding customer need is to have a single pane interface that is configurable for exactly what the customer needs. A refeshing user-centric approach, to turn down the noise, whilst leveraging existing systems.
What inspired you to do this?
Prior to starting a career in IT, Marten studied Visual Communications Design and was always fascinated by good design and great user experience. He is excited and inspired by the phenomenal changes that cloud is creating and the inspiring spirit and development contributions of the open source community. Currently completing Startup Engineering at Stanford University, is both an inspiration and platform to create an open source project and startup business to disrupt the cloud market. See more at Startup Engineering Stanford University.
What is this site for?
As a core requirement of the Stanford University Startup Engineering course, we are asking for your support both to help initially fund the business and open source project. The final graded component of the Stanford University course, requires students to build the web application and run a crowdfunder to attract pre-orders and/or social media attention for the startup product.
What are you going to do with the money?
As a crowfund activity, the funds will be used to accelerate the development of both the open source project and move the business beyond early stage. We are asking for your support to pay for additonal developers to build the web application, which will run on any Operating System on PC, Mac, Tablet or Smartphone. The source code will be published on GitHub, we will also develop commercial plugins and APIs to interface with OpenStack Horizon, Ansible and Rackspace. Funds will also be used to generate community development activity, to further the open source development of Dashscape. We Are Open to future discussions with Seed and Venture Capital Investors.
How long will this take?
As an early stage crowdfunded startup we need to move swiftly. Provided we meet our required target of A$50,000 within 8 weeks, we anticipate releasing the first open source software release and required plugin by November 2013. This will allow us to launch and present the software at the OpenStack Hong Kong Summit, where Marten Hauville has requested a place as a Speaker. If you are involved in the OpenStack Community, you can vote here. We will publish a Roadmap shortly, as our plan is over the next 12 months to move from current Early Stage Startup with intial software release, to Round A Funding with at least 2 major releases of the software. As well as rapid development, we will take our product to market vigorously across a wide sales channel.
