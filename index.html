FAQ

What does it do?
Enables clear control of multiple cloud environments, with individual saved views. Maintains simplicity through leveraging existing systems and a modular plugin approach.
What do I need it for?
Minimise multiple complicated interfaces, centralise monitoring for helpdesk (flightdeck), enable users for self-orchestration...
Who are we?
Dashscape is an Open Source project initiated by Marten Hauville and buildpartner. Experience of more than 20 years in IT industry, over 8 years building and delivering virtual environments, applications and managed services has identified the need to make hybrid and private cloud management easier.
What is your experience?
Marten Hauville successfully delivered business technology solutions across multiple business areas, working with product development, infrastructure engineers, developers and C level decision makers. Designing, building and delivering infrastructure, systems and applications for the enterprise market, for customers such as Cisco, HSBC, ING, Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA), Australian Securities Exchange (ASX), Telstra and SingTel Optus with experience as an Engineer, Architect, Project Manager and Technical Business Manager. See more at Marten Hauville on LinkedIn.
Why are you doing this?
For the past year Marten has been building OpenStack private clouds in Europe and Australia, as well as working with Rackspace and AWS public cloud environments. One thing is clear, the interfaces and management tools for these environments are unbearably complicated and disparate, despite the many commercial platforms out there. It doesn't need to be this way. The resounding customer need is to have a single pane interface that is configurable for exactly what the customer needs. A refeshing user-centric approach, to turn down the noise, whilst leveraging existing systems.
What inspired you to do this?
Prior to starting a career in IT, Marten studied Visual Communications Design and was always fascinated by good design and great user experience. He is excited and inspired by the phenomenal changes that cloud is creating and inspired by the spirit and enthusiasm of development contributions and the open source community. Currently completing Startup Engineering at Stanford University, is both an inspiration and platform to create an open source project and startup business to disrupt the cloud market. See more at Startup Engineering Stanford University.

What is cloud and what is Hybrid Cloud?
Simply put: the future of computing. Here is a basic overview which explains in plain language. Cloud incorporates many areas, where we are focusing is the IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service) Hybrid Cloud market initially. Hybrid Cloud is the use of more than one public cloud provider (AWS, Rackspace, Google Compute Engine), or mixed public, private and hosted/VPS environments. It enables organisations (when used with the right management and orchestration interface) to dynamically and automatically migrate operating systems from one provider cloud to another, usually based on cost (financial, latency, availability) and load requirements (CPU, memory, network, storage). A recent Gartner report forecast the IaaS market is expected to grow 47.3 percent in 2013 to $9 billion. There is also substantial forecast growth in this Hybrid Cloud area, particularly in enabling enterprise users on mobile devices.
Would it work with generic VPS servers or only with some sort of specific Hybrid Cloud?
Yes, Dashscape will work with your VPS servers and have cPanel and Plesk control panel support. Initially support will be for OpenStack Horizon and Rackspace control panel, then for other public cloud providers such as AWS and GCE management consoles. The main Dashscape feature Plugin available from the outset will be to Ansible, enabling true management, monitoring and orchestration from a single pane via SSH without the need for any agents. Typical orchestration requires agents installed on the target system during initial system build and is therefore complicated. These current Hybrid Cloud managment solutions offer a terrible and unnecessarily complicated user interface. With Dashscape you will be able to manage across multiple provider and Hybrid Cloud environments, simply and elegantly.
